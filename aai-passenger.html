DEL maintains dominance
Delhi grew from 25.8M to 79.3M (7.27% CAGR), holding ~30% market share throughout. Crossed 73.7M in FY2023-24 — largest single airport in India by a wide margin.
HYD + BLR outpace the field
Hyderabad (9.84% CAGR, 4.49× growth) and Bengaluru (9.23% CAGR, 4.11× growth) are the standout performers — both significantly outgrowing DEL and BOM over the 16-year period.
BOM losing relative share
Mumbai grew at only 5.25% CAGR — slowest of the top 4 airports. Its share of 8-airport traffic fell from 27.3% in FY2008-09 to 20.8% in FY2024-25 as BLR and HYD gained ground.

South India growth story
HYD (9.84%) and BLR (9.23%) are both South Indian tech hubs. Their superior CAGR reflects India's IT sector boom, rising business travel and fast-growing middle class in Telangana and Karnataka.
BOM capacity constrained
Mumbai's 5.25% CAGR — well below the group average — likely reflects CSIA's single-runway operational constraints and slot scarcity rather than weak demand. NAVI Mumbai airport should unlock pent-up growth.
CCJ — niche Gulf market
Calicut's 4.61% CAGR is the lowest in the group. Heavily dependent on Gulf VFR/labour traffic, it lacks the domestic business travel base that drives DEL/BLR/HYD growth. Highly exposed to Gulf migration policy changes.

BOM hit hardest
Mumbai fell 75.8% to just 11.1M in FY2020-21. Its high international traffic share (>35%) amplified the impact vs domestic-heavy airports. BOM also had the slowest post-COVID recovery pace.
HYD + BLR most resilient
HYD declined least at -63.1% and has surged furthest above pre-COVID levels (+34.6%). BLR is +29.3% above pre-COVID. Both driven by tech sector growth and strong domestic demand post-reopening.
MAA + CCU still lagging
Chennai is only 0.4% above pre-COVID peak and Kolkata is 0.9% below. Both markets show weaker post-COVID demand recovery vs national peers — potentially reflecting slower economic recovery in their catchment areas.
