Key insights

EU
EU is the faster-growing corridor
EU pax grew 88% in just 3 years (2022→2025) vs 49.5% for North America — a CAGR of 23.4%, nearly double the NA rate of 14.3%. Demand is accelerating, not plateauing.
North America
US dominates, but Canada is gaining
USA pax grew from 630K → 941K (+49%). Canada grew from 80K → 121K (+51%), outpacing US growth rate. Toronto (YYZ) is the #5 NA destination overall from BLR.
EU
Germany is the #1 EU market by far
Germany commands ~30% of all BLR-EU traffic (235K pax in 2025). Frankfurt (FRA) alone is the single largest EU destination. France and Netherlands are distant #2 and #3.
North America
SFO is the dominant NA gateway
San Francisco accounts for ~37% of all BLR-NA traffic — powered by the tech diaspora. JFK (#2) and DFW/ORD (#3/#4) trail significantly. BLR-SFO is the only meaningful direct route.
Both markets
Fares under pressure despite volume growth
EU avg fares fell from $448 → $392 (2023→2025). NA fell from $733 → $659. Volume growth is demand-driven and fare-sensitive — not a premium market trend.
Both markets
Gulf hubs remain dominant — but NA dependency is declining
Gulf carriers (EK+QR+EY) carry 38% of NA traffic (down from 48% in 2022) and ~42% of EU traffic. DXB and DOH are the top two transit hubs. European carriers (LH, AF, KLM) are growing share.
EU
Emerging EU markets growing explosively
Poland +82%, Czechia +200%, Croatia +155%, Malta +152%, Athens +110% since 2023. Eastern and Southern Europe are underpenetrated but accelerating — driven by new transit connectivity.
North America
Secondary US cities surging
Austin (AUS) grew +49% since 2023, Tampa +84%, San Diego +54%, Las Vegas +53%. Tech-hub and lifestyle city growth beyond the traditional NY/SF/Chicago triangle is notable.
Both markets
Almost no direct connectivity — massive opportunity
Only 17.4% of BLR-EU traffic is direct (mostly LHR); EU is 82.6% indirect. For NA, just 4.2% is direct (BLR-SFO only). Every passenger is a connection opportunity for BLR as a hub.
EU
Lufthansa leads EU carriers from BLR
Lufthansa carries 626K pax (all years) on BLR-EU routes — more than any airline including Gulf carriers. Air France (213K) and KLM (182K) follow. European carriers are BLR's EU backbone.
