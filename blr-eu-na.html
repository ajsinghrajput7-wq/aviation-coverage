Key insights
EU
EU is the faster-growing corridor
EU pax grew 88% in 3 years (2022→2025) vs 49.5% for NA — a CAGR of 23.4%, nearly double the NA rate. Demand is accelerating.
North America
US dominates, Canada is gaining
USA pax grew 630K→941K (+49%). Canada grew 80K→121K (+51%), outpacing US growth. Toronto (YYZ) is the #5 NA destination from BLR.
EU
Germany is the #1 EU market by far
Germany commands ~30% of all BLR-EU traffic (235K pax in 2025). Frankfurt alone is the single largest EU destination.
North America
SFO is the dominant NA gateway
San Francisco accounts for ~37% of all BLR-NA traffic — powered by the tech diaspora. Only meaningful direct route from BLR.
Both markets
Fares under pressure despite volume growth
EU avg fares fell from $448 → $392 (2023→2025). NA fell from $733 → $659. Volume-driven, not premium-driven growth.
Both markets
Almost no direct connectivity — massive opportunity
Only 17.4% of BLR-EU traffic is direct. For NA, just 4.2% is direct (BLR-SFO only). Every passenger is a hub connection opportunity.
EU
Emerging EU markets growing explosively
Poland +82%, Czechia +200%, Croatia +155%, Malta +152%, Athens +110% since 2023. Eastern Europe is underpenetrated but accelerating fast.
North America
Secondary US cities surging
Austin +49%, Tampa +84%, San Diego +54%, Las Vegas +53%. Tech-hub and lifestyle city growth beyond NY/SF/Chicago is notable.
EU
Lufthansa leads EU carriers from BLR
Lufthansa carries 626K pax (all years) on BLR-EU routes — more than any airline including Gulf carriers. AF (213K) and KLM (182K) follow.
Both markets
Gulf hub dependency declining in NA
Gulf carriers (EK+QR+EY) carry 38% of NA traffic (down from 48% in 2022). European carriers LH, AF, KLM are growing share steadily.
