Delhi is mission-critical
BLR-DEL (2.0M, 11.7% of airport) is 1.3× bigger than BOM and generates $162M — more than BLR's entire US market revenue. Any disruption to this route has outsized impact on total BLR performance.
Southern short-haul compression
MAA ($31), IXE ($32), GOI ($37), CCJ ($30), COK ($43) all show low fares. Short sectors with strong rail/road competition drive price suppression. These are likely high-frequency, leisure/VFR travellers.
Eastern corridor strength
CCU (837K), GAU (319K), BBI (303K), PAT (294K), IXB (253K) show BLR's strong east India links. GAU ($89) and IXB ($93) are the highest-yield domestic routes behind DEL ($81).

UAE: volume without yield
UAE (DXB+AUH+SHJ) = 428K pax but avg fare only $152. Revenue of $65M despite being the top-volume international market. Strong labour/VFR demand suppresses yield — nearly identical to Sri Lanka's $62 fare at the short-haul end.
USA: low volume, dominant revenue
471K pax (14.3% of intl) but $369M revenue (34% of intl revenue). Avg fare $782 is 5× the overall airport avg. BLR-SFO alone at $81M is the top single international revenue route.
UK: best large-market yield
215K pax, $80M revenue, $374 avg fare. LHR ($381 fare, 165K pax) = $63M alone. UK is BLR's highest-yield market at scale — more revenue-per-pax than UAE despite 2× fewer passengers.

Silicon Valley corridor
SFO (91K, $883) + SEA (29K, $770) + AUS (11K, $698) = 131K IT hub pax, $110M revenue. SFO alone is BLR's single highest revenue international route.
East Coast business
JFK (49K), ORD (26K), IAD (23K), BOS (22K) = 120K pax, ~$84M revenue. Strong corporate/consulting travel base with avg fares $575–$715.
Long-tail US depth
BLR connects to 50+ US cities. Small airports like IAH ($1,033), XNA ($1,417), ICT ($1,505) command premium fares from multi-stop routings. Long-tail contributes ~$50M+ additional revenue.
Strategic overview
Traffic concentration risk

Top 5 routes (DEL, BOM, CCU, HYD, PNQ) account for 33.4% of all BLR passengers. DEL alone = 11.7%. High concentration creates vulnerability to disruptions on a small number of metro corridors.

Pax share vs revenue share — the asymmetry

International = 19.3% of pax but 55.3% of revenue. Revenue-per-pax premium of international over domestic is 5.2×. Protecting international yield — especially USA, UK, Germany, Australia — is critical to BLR's revenue base.

Domestic: 80.7% pax, 44.7% rev. International: 19.3% pax, 55.3% rev.
Corridor findings
DEL is irreplaceable
BLR-DEL at 2.0M pax and $162M is BLR's single biggest economic corridor — nearly 2× BOM. More revenue than the entire US market. Capacity constraints here would have network-wide impact.
Middle East yield dilution
UAE + Saudi = 621K pax, avg fare $168. Revenue quality is low — UAE's $152 fare is just 2.4× domestic. Strong labour/VFR volume but weak premium mix constrains revenue growth.
UK — best yield at scale
215K pax, $80M, $374 avg. LHR at $381 fare is BLR's premium long-haul benchmark. Revenue-per-pax is 5.9× domestic vs UAE's 2.4×. Far more economically efficient corridor.
Australia — hidden strength
97K pax, $56M, $573 avg fare — 3rd highest yield country. Strong diaspora + professional travel. Revenue-per-pax nearly matches Japan ($502) and Canada ($497), well above its volume share.
Germany — corporate backbone
119K pax, $49M, $410 avg fare. BLR's German engineering and IT corporate base drives 4th-highest international revenue despite modest volume. FRA + MUC + BER together = strong premium corridor.
Thailand — leisure volume play
260K pax to Thailand (BKK + HKT + others) but only $105 avg fare and $27M revenue. Highest-volume SE Asia market but lowest yield. Growth here adds pax but limited revenue impact.
